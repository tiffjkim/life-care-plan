Gap funding sources

Check off sources as you research. Your subsidy gap from the rental scenario:

Cambridge city programs

  • Home Improvement Program (HIP)
    Low-interest & deferred-payment rehab loans for owner-occupants of 1-4 units. CDBG funded. 80% AMI limit. Contact: homeownership@cambridgema.gov / 617-349-4622
    Varies
  • Cambridge Affordable Housing Trust
    City trust fund for affordable housing creation and preservation. Soft loans or grants.
    Varies
  • Lead paint abatement assistance
    State programs for deleading. Required before renting to families with children under 6.
    Up to $20K

State programs (Massachusetts)

  • MassHousing — Home Improvement Loan Program
    Below-market loans for owner-occupants of 1-4 units. Covers major systems.
    Up to $50K
  • Mass Save — Energy efficiency rebates
    Rebates for insulation, heat pumps, weatherization. Available to all building owners.
    $5K-$15K
  • EOHLC — HOME Investment Partnership funds
    Federal block grant. Must maintain affordability 5-20 years.
    Up to $40K/unit
  • CEDAC — Predevelopment loans
    Early-stage financing if partnering with a nonprofit developer or CLT.
    Varies

Federal programs

  • USDA Section 504 Repair Loans & Grants
    Loans up to $40K, grants up to $10K for very low-income homeowners (50% AMI).
    $10K-$40K
  • FHA 203(k) Rehabilitation Mortgage
    Combines purchase + renovation into single mortgage. Owner-occupants of 1-4 units.
    Loan product

Other sources

  • Community land trust partnership
    Cambridge CLT or Homeowner's Rehab Inc may partner to unlock subsidies.
    Partnership
  • CDFI loans
    Below-market financing from mission-driven lenders. Typically 2-4% below conventional.
    Below-mkt
  • Solar tax credits + Mass SMART
    Federal 30% ITC for solar. MA SMART provides per-kWh payments.
    30% ITC
  • Historic preservation tax credits
    If in historic district: MA 20% + federal 20% for certified rehab.
    20% of rehab

Strategy: Start with Cambridge HIP and Mass Save (easiest). Then FHA 203(k) for combined financing. Stack sources to close the gap.
